Bio

My name is Julia Chen and I am from Kensington, California. I earned a B.S. in Microbiology and Ecosystem Management and Forestry from University of California-Berkeley.

Prior to continuing my education at the Yale School of the Environment, my work in the Museum of Vertebrate Zoology at UC Berkeley involved mapping new amphibian species ranges and exploring the role of protected areas in amphibian conservation. In addition, I assisted in managing the specimen database ARCTOS for locality data accuracy. As a Pacific Forest Trust intern, I researched updated costs for forest and watershed management actions as well as valuation of ecosystem benefits in California.

Here at Yale School of Environment, my focus is on forest management issues in the Western United States. I am interested in improving stakeholder engagement for the sustainable stewardship of forestlands. I also want to develop robust management scenario simulations that tailor towards the distinct needs of private landowners and public land entities.

After earning a Master of Forestry, I hope to work for the US Forest Service and private industry to expose myself to applied work in a wide range of sectors. I intend to become a registered professional forester as the need increases with forest health crises.
